## It Started With Ollie

Some businesses start with a business plan. Ours started with a road trip.

In 2017, Gus and Marian closed on their first home, a 1968 garrison colonial on a quiet street in Sharon with the big yard they had always dreamed about. Less than a month later, they were in the car driving to Virginia to pick up a puppy named Ollie. On the way back, they stopped in New York City. Somewhere in Central Park, with Ollie bounding through the grass and the city humming around them, something clicked. They didn't have a plan yet. They had Ollie, and that turned out to be enough.

---

## From One Dog to Many

A few months into puppyhood — potty training, crate training, the whole adventure — a close friend needed help. He was traveling and asked if Gus and Marian could look after his dog. They said yes without hesitation.

What happened next changed everything.

Watching Ollie run, play, and socialize alongside another dog in a safe, familiar environment was unlike anything they had experienced with Ollie before. The joy was immediate and obvious. The next day, Gus opened a Rover profile.

For five years, Paws on Longmeadow operated quietly and part-time. Word spread the way it does in a neighborhood: slowly, then all at once. Families came back. They referred their friends. The dogs kept coming.

In 2024, Gus made it official. He left his career as a creative project manager and committed fully to the work he had already fallen in love with.

## This Is Our Home

That's not a tagline. It's the most literal thing we can say.

Paws on Longmeadow is not a facility. It is not a kennel. It is not a commercial operation that happens to have nice photos. It is the home where Gus and Marian live, where their kids are growing up, and where Ollie, now 9 years old, holds court from his favorite spot.

When your dog stays with us, they are sleeping where we sleep, playing where our family plays, and being watched over the way we watch over our own.

## The People Behind the Paws

**Gus** spent years working as a creative project manager before making Paws on Longmeadow his full-time life in 2024. That background shows in the way things are run, in the attention to detail, in the consistency, in the reliability, in the experience he works hard to create for every family. 

**Marian** is, in many ways, the heartbeat of the day-to-day. With a background in design and a remote work schedule that keeps her home, she is a constant presence — greeting nearly every dog who walks through the door and saying goodbye to each one when they leave. She handles some walks, drives dogs home, and together with Gus, always makes sure every pup feels seen. She also takes the best photos and videos, a fact that becomes obvious the moment you see them. 

**Their two boys** have grown up in the middle of all of this. They have never known a home without dogs in it. The pups know it too.

## About Ollie

Ollie is the reason any of this exists.

At 9 years old, he has lived an extraordinary life — hundreds of friends made, countless adventures had, and one very serious chapter that tested all of us. A few years ago, Ollie suffered a ruptured disc which caused serious inflammation and compression of the spinal cord and required surgery. His mobility today is limited.

Ollie moves through this house with the quiet authority of someone who knows exactly what he built. Every new dog meets him first, and he still takes the job seriously.
