# Our Home

## The Quiet Before the Storm

Most mornings start the same way. Marian is up early, coffee in hand, out on Ollie's Deck before the first dog arrives. She sets the space, checks the water station, takes a breath. It is the quiet before the storm — and she loves it.

By the time the first daycare dog comes through the gate, everything is ready.

This is a home first. A garrison colonial on a quiet Sharon street where Gus and Marian live, where their boys are growing up, and where Ollie has held court for nine years. The stillness Marian sets each morning is the same stillness the dogs come home to at the end of the day.

---

## Ollie's Deck

Every dog that comes through our doors lands here first.

Ollie's Deck is a large outdoor space that serves as the landing point between the house, the yard, and the street. It is where dogs arrive, decompress, and transition between the different parts of their day. Outfitted with Kuranda elevated beds, a water station, and plenty of shade, it is as functional as it is comfortable.

Three access points keep everything organized: a door into the main level of the house, a gate into the backyard, and a gate that connects to the street for curbside drop-off and pickup. It is the first thing your dog experiences when they arrive and the last thing they see before they go home.

## The Yard

The backyard is fully fenced and measures approximately 12,000 square feet. Plenty of room to run, but not enough to get lost.

The ground is a mix of grass, gravel, and natural mulch cover, giving dogs a variety of surfaces to move across and explore. The yard gets partial sun throughout the day, with mature trees providing natural shade. There is always somewhere cool to settle.

Fencing is six feet tall across most of the perimeter, a combination of vinyl and chainlink, with three secure gates that keep arrivals, departures, and yard access organized and controlled at all times.

We treat the yard with organic fertilizer and maintain a regular mosquito and tick spray schedule, using products that are safe for dogs. The yard is cleaned daily. What your dog plays in matters to us.

## The Inside

During the day, dogs have access to the main level of our home. The living room, the kitchen, the places a family actually lives. There is a baby gate at the stairs to keep things contained to the first floor, and the vibe tends to be calm, cozy, and unhurried indoors and active outside.

Dogs sleep wherever they are most comfortable. Kuranda elevated beds are set up on the main level. There is a couch that has become very popular. Some dogs bring their own crate and bedding from home, which we always welcome. For boarding dogs, feeding happens inside crates or behind an x-pen. It is a simple practice that eliminates resource guarding and keeps mealtimes calm and safe for everyone.

We keep a selection of safe large crates on hand, including impact crates and rock creek crates, for clients who prefer not to bring their own. An oversized play pen is also available for temporary separation from the group or to encourage rest when a dog needs a quiet moment.

On days when the heat is extreme or the rain is heavy, dogs have access to our climate controlled lower level. It does not come up often, but when it does, everyone stays comfortable.

We tend not to have chronic barkers in the group. The house stays quieter than you might expect.

## How Arrivals Work

Curbside drop-off and pickup keep things safe and orderly for every dog on the property. Here is what we ask:

**Dropping off:** Send us a text when you are on your way and again when you arrive. We will meet you at the curb. Please do not enter the driveway or property.

**Picking up:** Same process. A heads up text and an ETA go a long way, especially on busy days. We will have your dog ready and waiting.

For boarding arrivals, bring your dog's food, a blanket, and one or two familiar items from home. A bathroom walk before you arrive is always a good idea. From Ollie's Deck, we introduce new arrivals to the existing group in a controlled, unhurried way. No rushed first meetings. We pay attention to how every dog enters a space.

## Designed With Purpose

Becoming a licensed, permitted operation gave us something valuable: the opportunity to be intentional about how this space works.

We are all about creating distinct environments that give us more control over the experience each dog has. Separate areas for different energy levels, play styles, and group dynamics. A space that is thoughtfully arranged, not just available.

Every change we make is guided by our operating permit and the standards set by our local Animal Control Officer. That accountability is not a burden. It is exactly the kind of structure a home-based operation should have.
