## Why This Law Exists

Ollie's Law is named after a dog.

In 2020, a seven-month-old labradoodle named Ollie was severely injured by other dogs at a Massachusetts boarding facility. He spent two months in veterinary care before he died of his injuries, and his owner was never given a clear answer about how it happened. Her fight for accountability became Ollie's Law, which Governor Healey signed in 2024.

We take that seriously. Not because we are required to, but because it is exactly the kind of accountability that should exist in this industry. We have a dog named Ollie too. We know what it means to love one.

## What Ollie's Law Means for Us

Paws on Longmeadow is fully compliant with Ollie's Law. We operate under a Special Permit from the Town of Sharon Zoning Board of Appeals, hold a valid kennel license, and maintain complete health and compliance records for every dog in our care. Our property is inspected annually by the Town of Sharon Animal Control Officer.

## What It Means for You

**Ollie's Law & What It Means for You**

On September 20, 2024, Governor Maura Healey signed the Act to Increase Kennel Safety, commonly known as Ollie's Law. This law took effect on December 19, 2024, and applies to Paws on Longmeadow along with all daycare and boarding facilities throughout Massachusetts. Here is a simple breakdown of what is new and what we will need from you.

**Rabies Vaccination Certificate** — We are required to keep a current Rabies vaccination certificate on file for every dog in our care. A general note from your veterinarian with only the vaccine date and expiration is no longer sufficient. We will need the full certificate. Please contact your vet's office if you need a copy.

**Dog License / Registration** — Massachusetts has always required all dogs over six months of age to be individually licensed with their city or town, and Ollie's Law now requires facilities like ours to maintain a record of that valid license or registration. If you have not licensed your dog yet, please reach out to your local town or city clerk's office to get that sorted.

**Collar & Tags** — All dogs must wear a collar or harness while in our care, and it must display both a current Rabies tag and an inscribed municipal dog tag.

**Questions?** For more detail on the law, visit the state's official Ollie's Law page at [mass.gov](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/ollies-law-information), or reach out to your local Animal Control Officer with any specific questions. As always, thank you for trusting us with your furry family members.
