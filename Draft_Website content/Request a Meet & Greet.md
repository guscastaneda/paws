## Every Great Stay Starts Here

Before any booking, every new client at Paws on Longmeadow starts with a Meet & Greet. It's free, it's relaxed, and it's the most important step we take together.

This isn't a formality. It's how we make sure your dog is set up for a great experience. And it's how you leave feeling confident about the people you're trusting with your pup.

---

## What to Expect

**A real conversation** — We'll walk through everything. The services you're interested in, your dog's daily routine, their quirks, their triggers, what they love, and what makes them nervous. We want to know your dog the way you know your dog, before they ever spend a night with us.

We'll also share our story. How we got here, how we run things, and what we believe good dog care actually looks like. By the end, you'll know exactly who we are and how we operate.

**A look at your dog** — While we talk, we're also paying attention. How your dog carries themselves, how they respond to new people and a new environment, what their energy tells us. We're looking for cues, not red flags. But if something tells us the fit isn't right, we'll be honest with you about that too. It goes both ways.

**Where it happens** — If you're interested in boarding, daycare, or any service provided at our home, the Meet & Greet takes place on our deck. You'll get a feel for the property, the space, and the environment your dog would be coming into. There will likely be other dogs inside the house during your visit. They won't interact with your dog at this stage, but you'll hear them, and that's intentional. We want your dog's first impression to be calm and controlled.

If you're interested in house sitting, drop-in visits, or walks, we come to you. The Meet & Greet happens at your home, on your dog's turf, where they're most comfortable.

See the space → [[Our Home]]

**How long it takes** — Usually 30 to 45 minutes. Never rushed.

**What happens after** — If it's a great fit, and it usually is, we schedule a trial stay before any extended booking. The trial is your dog's first real visit. A low-stakes run where they get comfortable with us and we get comfortable with them. After that, you're a Paws on Longmeadow family.

You'll also receive a personal booking link by email. Your direct line for all future requests, so you never have to start from scratch again. That link takes you directly to → [[Book Us]].

### Step 4 — Vaccination Records

Before your dog's first visit, we require current documentation for:

- Rabies
- DHPP (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza)
- Bordetella, within the last 12 months
- Canine Influenza, strongly recommended

Please email records to **records@pawsonlongmeadow.com** ahead of your Meet & Greet. If you're unable to, bring printed or digital copies to your appointment.
