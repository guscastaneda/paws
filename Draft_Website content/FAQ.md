## Getting Started

### Do I need to do anything before my dog's first visit?
Yes — every new client starts with a free Meet & Greet. This is a no-pressure visit where we get to know you and your dog, walk through your dog's routine and needs, and make sure it's a great fit for everyone. From there, a trial stay is required before any regular booking. It's a low-commitment way to make sure your dog is comfortable and happy with us before you commit to a longer stay.

→ [[Request a Meet & Greet]]

### Why is a trial required?
Every dog is different, and we take the comfort and safety of every dog in our care seriously. The trial gives your dog a chance to get familiar with our home, our family, and the other pups in a low-stakes setting. Most dogs take to it immediately. And for the ones who need a little more time, it helps us figure out how to set them up for success.

### How do I schedule a Meet & Greet?
Just reach out through our [[Request a Meet & Greet]] page or [[Contact]] page and let us know you're a new client. We'll find a time that works and take it from there.

## Hours & Drop-Off

### What are your hours?
Monday through Saturday: 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM  
Sunday: 12:00 PM to 4:30 PM

### How does drop-off and pick-up work?
We use a curbside drop-off and pick-up system. Dogs are transferred at the curb — there is no entry onto the driveway or property for drop-off. This keeps things smooth, safe, and consistent for all the dogs in our care. It becomes second nature after your first visit.

See also → [[Our Home]] for a full description of how arrivals work.

### Can I arrange an early drop-off or late pick-up?
Our hours are set by our operating permit and we ask that all drop-offs and pick-ups happen within those windows. If you have a specific scheduling need, reach out and we'll do our best to accommodate.

## Health & Vaccination Requirements

### What vaccinations does my dog need?
All dogs must have current documentation for the following before their first visit:

- Rabies (current certificate required under Ollie's Law)
- DHPP (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza)
- Bordetella (Kennel Cough), within the last 12 months
- Canine Influenza (strongly recommended)

### Are there other ~~health~~ requirements?
Yes. All dogs must be:
- At least 6 months old
- Spayed or neutered (case-by-case exceptions may be considered)
- Free of fleas, ticks, and parasites
- Feeling well on the day of their visit — dogs showing signs of illness such as vomiting, diarrhea, or coughing will not be accepted
- Wearing a secure collar with a rabies vaccine tag and a current town license tag from their town of residence. 

See also → [[Ollie's Law]] for additional documentation requirements under Massachusetts law.

## During the Stay

### What does a typical day look like?
Your dog spends the day in and around our home. There's playtime with other dogs, walks, rest time, and plenty of attention from Gus and Marian. We keep things active but not overwhelming, and we pay close attention to each dog's energy level and social preferences throughout the day.

See the full facility description → [[Our Home]]

### Can I bring food or personal items from home?
Yes, with some limits. We require your dog's own food. A familiar comfort item like a blanket is fine too. We ask that you keep it simple to avoid anything getting lost or damaged during the stay.

### How often will I get updates?
Frequently. Photos and videos are a signature of how we operate — our clients have come to expect them and we love sending them. You won't be left wondering how your dog is doing.

### Do you accommodate dogs with medical needs?
Yes, within reason. We have experience managing daily medications, special diets, and dogs with physical limitations. Gus and Marian are trained in canine first aid and keep a fully stocked pet first-aid kit on site. If your dog has specific medical needs, tell us during the Meet & Greet and we'll let you know if we're the right fit.

## Safety & Emergencies

### What happens if my dog gets sick or injured during a stay?
We follow a clear emergency protocol:

1. We contact you immediately.
2. If you can't be reached, your service agreement authorizes us to seek emergency veterinary care on your dog's behalf, up to a $500 pre-authorized limit per incident by default. You can set a higher limit during onboarding.
3. Where the situation allows, we reach out to your dog's own veterinarian first, since they know your dog's history best.
4. Our designated providers are Sharon Veterinary Clinic (781-784-7554) locally, Foxboro Animal Hospital (508-543-5350) for daytime non-emergencies, and Tufts Veterinary Emergency Treatment and Specialties in Walpole (508-668-5454) for 24-hour emergencies, roughly 15 to 20 minutes from our home.

You will never be left out of the loop.

### Is the yard secure?
Yes. Our outdoor space is fully fenced and inspected annually by the Town of Sharon Animal Control Officer as a condition of our operating permit. Any fencing issues are repaired immediately.

See also → [[Our Home]] for yard details.

## Booking & Payment

### How do I request a stay?
New clients start with a Meet & Greet → [[Request a Meet & Greet]].  
Returning clients request directly through their personal booking link → [[Book Us]].

### How does payment work?
We invoice weekly, and payment is due on receipt. New clients are invoiced after their first stay, so there's no upfront payment to get started, aside from a peak season deposit where one applies.

### Do you require a deposit for bookings?
Yes. Peak season reservations require a 50% deposit to hold your dates, applied to your final invoice. If you cancel, the deposit is applied against any cancellation amount owed under our peak season policy, and anything left over is credited or refunded. See the cancellation policy on [[Pricing]] for the peak season notice windows.

### What is your cancellation policy?
The short version: cancel daycare 24 or more hours ahead, or boarding 7 or more days ahead, and there is no charge. Cancel inside those windows and a fee applies, partial for boarding, full for daycare, and a no-show is always charged in full. Peak season windows are wider. Cancel within 4 hours of booking and you are never charged. Full terms are on [[Pricing]].

### When are peak season dates?
Peak season follows the school calendar and major holidays. See the full schedule on → [[Pricing]].

## About the Business

### Is Paws on Longmeadow licensed and permitted?
Yes. We operate under a Special Permit from the Town of Sharon Zoning Board of Appeals and comply with all Massachusetts state kennel licensing requirements. Our property is inspected annually by the Town of Sharon Animal Control Officer.

### What is Ollie's Law?
Ollie's Law is Massachusetts legislation establishing standards for pet care businesses operating in the state. We are fully compliant. Learn more → [[Ollie's Law]].

### How many dogs do you take at once?
Group sizes are determined in accordance with Massachusetts state regulations and the guidance of our local Animal Control Officer. We comply fully and prioritize safety above all else.
