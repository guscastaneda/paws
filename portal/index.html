Paws on Longmeadow, LLC — Client Service Agreement

Sharon, Massachusetts · Version 1.0

This Agreement is entered into between Paws on Longmeadow, LLC ("the Company") and the client identified below ("the Client"). It governs all services provided by the Company now and in the future.

1. Authority

The Client represents they are at least 18 years of age, the lawful owner of the Pet or duly authorized agent, and have full authority to enter into this Agreement and authorize veterinary treatment.

2. Health & Vaccination Requirements

The Pet's vaccinations must be current at all times. Required vaccinations: Rabies (certificate required per Ollie's Law), DHPP, and Bordetella within the last 12 months. The Pet must hold a valid municipal dog license and wear a collar bearing a current Rabies tag and dog license tag at all times while in the Company's care. The Client shall disclose any medications, allergies, medical conditions, or special needs in writing prior to each booking.

3. Behavioral Requirements

All pets are subject to a temperament evaluation prior to or during their first visit. The Company reserves the right to refuse or discontinue service to any Pet displaying aggression or behavior posing a risk to animals, staff, or property. All new pets are required to complete a trial stay before any extended booking. The Client must disclose all known behavioral issues including aggression, separation anxiety, resource guarding, escape history, or bite history prior to the first visit.

4. Emergency Veterinary Authorization

If the Pet requires veterinary care and the Client cannot be reached, the Client authorizes the Company to seek and consent to veterinary treatment on the Pet's behalf, including diagnostics, treatment, and humane euthanasia where medically necessary. The Company will make reasonable efforts to contact the Client before authorizing expenditures exceeding $500. The Client accepts full financial responsibility for all veterinary costs incurred.

5. Animal Interaction & Liability

The Client acknowledges that pet care environments involve inherent risks including bites, scratches, and injuries that can occur even with reasonable supervision. The Client accepts full liability for any injury, damage, or loss caused by the Pet to other animals, persons, or property while in the Company's care. Services are provided in the Company's private residence, which may include the Company's own pets and family members.

6. Payment Terms

The Company invoices weekly via Venmo Business or such other method as agreed. Payment is due upon receipt. Peak season reservations require a 25% deposit. Late balances over 14 days may incur a $25 late fee. A $35 fee applies to returned payments or chargebacks.

7. Cancellation Policy

Daycare: Cancellations must be received at least 24 hours in advance. Cancellations under 24 hours are charged 50% of the daycare rate. No-shows are charged the full rate.

Boarding: Cancellations more than 48 hours before the start date incur no charge. Cancellations under 48 hours are charged one night's boarding rate. No-shows are charged the full reservation amount.

Peak Season: Cancellations 7 or more days before the start date receive a full credit toward a future stay. Cancellations under 7 days forfeit the deposit.

8. Photo & Video Release

The Client grants the Company permission to photograph and video record the Pet during services and to use such materials for business purposes including the Company's website and social media. The Client's name and identifying information will not be used without separate written consent. This release may be revoked at any time by written notice.

9. Limitation of Liability

To the fullest extent permitted by Massachusetts law, the Company's liability for any claim arising out of services provided under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by the Client for the specific service giving rise to the claim. The Company shall not be liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages. Nothing in this section limits liability for gross negligence or willful misconduct.

10. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved by binding arbitration administered under the rules of the American Arbitration Association. The Client waives the right to a jury trial and the right to participate in any class action. Nothing herein prevents either party from seeking emergency injunctive relief.

11. General

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties. It may be amended only by written agreement signed by both parties. Electronic signatures have the same legal effect as original signatures pursuant to M.G.L. c. 110G. If any provision is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force.

This is a summary of the key terms of the Paws on Longmeadow Client Service Agreement. The full agreement including all Service-Specific Addendums is available upon request. By signing below you confirm you have read and agree to all terms.
